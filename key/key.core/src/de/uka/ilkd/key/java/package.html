This package contains classes that cover the Java programming language. The classes in the subpackages are mainly taken from the Recoder framework and made immutable. They are transformed into this data structure from a Recoder structure by {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Recoder2KeY} or {@link de.uka.ilkd.key.SchemaRecoder2KeY}. However, in some details both data structures might differ more. The following explanations are adapted from the documentation of the Recoder framework.
Source and Program Elements
A {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.SourceElement} is a syntactical entity and not necessary a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ModelElement}, such as a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Comment}.

A {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ProgramElement} is a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.SourceElement} and a {@link de.uka.ilkd.key.ModelElement}. It is aware of its parent in the syntax tree, while a pure {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.SourceElement} is not considered as a member of the AST even though it is represented in the sources.

{@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ProgramElement}s are further classified into {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.TerminalProgramElement}s and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.NonTerminalProgramElement}s. While {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.TerminalProgramElement} is just a tag class, {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.NonTerminalProgramElement}s know their AST children (while it is possible that they do not have any). A complete source file occurs as a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.CompilationUnit}.

{@link org.key_project.common.core.services.JavaSourceElement} and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.JavaProgramElement} are abstract classes defining standard implementations that already know their {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.JavaProgramFactory}.

Expressions and Statements
{@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Expression} and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Statement} are self-explanatory. A {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.LoopInitializer} is a special {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Statement} valid as initializer of {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.statement.For} loops. {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.LoopInitializer} is subtyped by {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.expression.ExpressionStatement} and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.LocalVariableDeclaration}).

Concrete classes and further abstractions are bundled in the {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.expression} and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.statement} packages.

Syntax Tree Parents
There are a couple of abstractions dealing with properties of being a parent node.

These are {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.TypeDeclarationContainer}, {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ExpressionContainer}, {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.StatementContainer}, {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ParameterContainer}, {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.NamedProgramElement} and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.reference.TypeReferenceContainer}. A An {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ExpressionContainer} contains {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Expression}s, a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.StatementContainer} contains {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Statement}s, a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.ParameterContainer} (either a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.MethodDeclaration} or a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.statement.Catch} statement) contains {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.ParameterDeclaration}s. A {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.NamedProgramElement} is a subtype of {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.NamedModelElement}. A {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.reference.TypeReferenceContainer} contains one or several names, but these are names of types that are referred to explicitely by a {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.reference.TypeReference}.

References
A {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Reference} is an explicite use of an entity. Most of these {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Reference}s are {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.reference.NameReference}s and as such {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.NamedProgramElement}s, e.g. the {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.reference.TypeReference}. Subtypes of {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Reference}s are bundled in the {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.reference} package.

Modifiers and Declarations
{@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.Modifier}s are (exclusively) used in the context of {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Declaration}s. {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.Modifier}s occur explicitly, since they occur as syntactical tokens that might be indented and commented. {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Declaration}s are either declarations of types or other entities such as {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.MemberDeclaration} or {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.VariableDeclaration}. Concrete {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.Modifier}s and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.Declaration}s are bundled in the {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration.modifier} and {@link org.key_project.common.core.services.declaration} packages.
