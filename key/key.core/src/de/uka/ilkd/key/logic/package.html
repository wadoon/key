provides a representation for the term and sequent structure. The structure of a term is defined in {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term}. Subclasses of {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term} provide representations for special kinds of terms. However, these subclasses are supposed to be not directly accessed. Instead, {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term} provides methods for all the methods of the subclasses. Moreover, {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term}s (or their subclasses) are supposed to be never constructed by constructors of their own, but by instances of {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.TermFactory}.

The function of {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term}s (e.g., if they represent a conjunction of subterms, a quantified formula,...) is only determined by an {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.op.Operator} that is assigned to a {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term} when the {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term} is constructed.

{@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Sequent}s consist of two {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Semisequent}s which represent a duplicate-free list of a {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.SequentFormula}s. The latter consist of a {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Constraint} and a {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term} of a special sort {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.sort.Sort#FORMULA}.

{@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Sequent}s and {@link org.key_project.common.core.logic.Term}s are immutable. Last modified: Wed Dec 4 15:11:14 MET 2002
